History & Framework

The intersection of biological regeneration, capital economics, and spatial logistics.

Project Introduction

This project models the complex bioeconomic dynamics of a national food system. While traditional models evaluate agriculture purely as an economic venture, real-world systems are fundamentally bound by environmental and social constraints.

By merging these disciplines, we analyze how agricultural biomass is generated under environmental limits while simultaneously tracking the economic capital and social equity of the state.

The objective is to progressively advance from static baseline Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) to highly complex, spatial Stochastic Partial Differential Equations (SPDEs)—evaluating dynamic policy feedback and supply chain logistics.

The systemic architecture utilizes the Method of Lines and Euler-Maruyama integration to numerically solve these spatial equations. This web application translates that quantitative mathematical foundation directly into an interactive, browser-based playground.

Mathematical Framework

Temporal Dynamics (The 4 ODEs)

The foundational model evaluates the system as a localized, well-mixed point. We define four linked state variables that balance production, environmental health, wealth, and fairness:

$$ \frac{dF}{dt} = \alpha C N u_p - \beta P - \gamma F $$

Food Production Dynamics: Tracks agricultural biomass generation versus population consumption and waste.

$$ \frac{dN}{dt} = r N \left(1 - \frac{N}{K}\right) - \delta F u_p + \sigma u_s $$

Natural Resource Health: Models logistic ecological regeneration against the degradation of aggressive industrial farming.

$$ \frac{dC}{dt} = p_{margin} (\beta P) - c F - (u_s + u_e) C $$

Economic Capital: Calculates the state GDP, funded by food sales and drained by production costs and policy intervention taxes.

$$ \frac{dEq}{dt} = k_1 u_e + k_2 \max(0, F - D) - \mu Eq $$

Equity Index: Measures social fairness, improving through active government funding or massive biological food surpluses.

Spatial Logistics & Market Shocks (The 2 SPDEs)

Real-world food systems are spatially heterogeneous. To account for physical supply chains and regional market shocks, we transition the highly mobile variables (Food and Capital) into SPDEs. We introduce the Laplacian operator (\( \nabla^2 \)) to model physical diffusion and a Gaussian white noise term (\( dW \)) to model unpredictable environmental volatility:

$$ dF = \left( \alpha C N u_p - \beta P - \gamma F + D_F \frac{\partial^2 F}{\partial x^2} \right) dt + \sigma_F F dW_F $$

Spatial Food Logistics: Simulates trucks moving surplus food across infrastructure (\( D_F \)) while weathering regional agricultural shocks.

$$ dC = \left( p_{margin} (\beta P) - c F - (u_s + u_e) C + D_C \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial x^2} \right) dt + \sigma_C C dW_C $$

Spatial Capital Flow: Simulates financial wealth flowing back to agrarian hubs (\( D_C \)) while enduring localized economic volatility.

The interactive implementation of these models can be explored within the Playground, where users can adjust policy parameters and observe the resulting system dynamics in real time.
